Abstract

The agricultural domain presents unique challenges for question-answering systems due to its specialized terminology, context-dependent decisions, and reliance on localized knowledge. While large language models (LLMs) excel at general reasoning tasks, their ability to handle domain-specific agricultural reasoning has not been sufficiently explored.

This work bridges that gap by introducing a dedicated dataset and a comprehensive benchmarking strategy tailored to agronomic reasoning tasks. Our key contributions include:

  • A curated collection of 55,000 agronomy-focused QA pairs enriched with reasoning traces, developed via an expert-in-the-loop pipeline to ensure relevance, quality, and clarity.
  • A novel evaluation framework that assesses reasoning capabilities across models, distinguishing between reasoning and non-reasoning LLMs.
  • A distilled lightweight model trained on the dataset, offering performance comparable to larger models while remaining efficient and scalable.
